🎸 The Guitar

Six strings. Infinite possibilities. My passion for guitar shapes how I think, create, and connect with the world.

Why Guitar?

Guitar isn't just a hobby — it's a discipline. Learning to play taught me patience, persistence, and the satisfaction of gradual mastery. The same qualities that make a great guitarist make a great professional: deliberate practice, attention to detail, and the ability to read the room.

A classical acoustic guitar — the foundation of most playing styles.

The Guitar Learning Path

Most guitarists follow a progression like this. Each stage unlocks new possibilities:

  1. Open Chords — The starting point for every guitarist.
    • E major and E minor
    • A major, A minor, and A7
    • G, C, D — the "campfire" chords
    • F major (the first real challenge)
  2. Barre Chords — Movable shapes that unlock the whole fretboard.
    • E-shape barre chord
    • A-shape barre chord
    • Minor and dominant 7th variations
  3. Scales & Lead Playing — Moving beyond chords.
    • Pentatonic minor scale (most used in rock)
    • Major scale and its modes
    • Blues scale
  4. Music Theory — Understanding why it works.
    • Key signatures and chord families
    • The CAGED system
    • Reading chord charts and tabs
